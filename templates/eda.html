TEMPERATURE

The lower the air density, the faster an airplane must travel to produce enough lift to take off. These hotter temperatures will reduce air density and make it much more likely weight restrictions are needed for flights taking off during the hottest parts of the day.




WIND

During flight, winds have an effect on the plane's speed, so they must be taken into consideration if the aircraft wants to stay on schedule. For instance, tailwinds make travel faster and save fuel, while headwinds have the opposite effect




CLOUDY

When an aircraft moves through clouds containing water vapor of a certain temperature, the sudden cooling caused by the passing of the aircraft, spurs the vapor to freeze into ice crystals, which then fall through lower clouds.




HUMIDITY

Many passengers experience discomfort during flight because of the effect of low humidity on the skin, eyes, throat, and nose. ... The low humidity at cruise altitude (minimum 22.7 %) was associated with a mean decrease in admittance of about 20 % compared with measures in the airport.
